Assignment  8:

 Case Study : Amazon-Style E-Commerce Order Management System

Business Context
You are working for an e-commerce company like Amazon. During the holiday season, the platform receives millions of orders. You need to build an order processing and analytics system.

 Problem Statement
Create a Java application to manage orders, calculate business metrics, detect fraud, and optimize inventory.

Given Data Structures

Product Class:
- productId (String)
- productName (String)
- category (String) - "Electronics", "Clothing", "Books", "Home", "Toys"
- price (double)
- stockQuantity (int)
- seller (String)
- rating (double)

Order Class:
orderId (String)
- customerId (String)
- productId (String)
- quantity (int)
- orderAmount (double)
- orderDate (LocalDateTime)
- status (String) - "PLACED", "SHIPPED", "DELIVERED", "CANCELLED", "RETURNED"
- paymentMode (String) - "CARD", "UPI", "COD", "WALLET"
- deliveryCity (String)
- isPrime (boolean) - Prime member or not

Customer Class:
- customerId (String)
- name (String)
- email (String)
- phoneNumber (String)
- city (String)
- isPrimeMember (boolean)
- registrationDate (LocalDate)

 Requirements

 Level 1: Order Analytics
1. Find total number of orders placed today
2. Calculate total revenue from all delivered orders
3. List all pending orders (status = "PLACED")
4. Find orders above ₹10,000
5. Count how many orders were cancelled
6. Calculate average order value

 Level 2: Customer Analytics
7. Find top 10 customers by total purchase amount
8. Calculate Prime vs Non-Prime customer revenue contribution
9. Identify customers who have returned more than 3 orders (potential fraud)
10. Find city-wise order distribution
11. Calculate customer lifetime value (total orders per customer)
12. Find customers who haven't ordered in last 30 days (inactive customers)

 Level 3: Product & Inventory Analytics
13. Find best-selling products (by quantity sold)
14. Calculate category-wise revenue
15. Identify low-stock products (stock < 10) that are high-selling
16. Find products with high return rate (>15% of orders returned)
17. Calculate average delivery time for each city
18. Payment mode preference analysis

Level 4: Advanced Business Intelligence
19. Fraud Detection:  Find customers with multiple orders from different cities on same day
20. Dynamic Pricing: Identify products with low ratings but high sales (pricing issue?)
21.  Inventory Optimization: Products with high stock but low sales in last 30 days
22. Prime Conversion Opportunity: Non-prime customers with >5 orders in last month
23. Seller Performance: Rank sellers by total revenue and average rating
24. Seasonal Trends: Compare current month sales with previous month (growth %)

Technical Hints
- Use `LocalDateTime` for order date filtering
- Use `Collectors.groupingBy()` with multiple levels
- Use `Collectors.summingDouble()` for revenue calculation
- Use `filter()` chains for complex conditions
- Use `Map<String, DoubleSummaryStatistics>` for statistics
- Use `sorted()` with custom comparators for ranking
